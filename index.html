About

I'm a computational scientist and R&D leader working at the intersection of machine learning and biology. At Bristol Myers Squibb I direct a team advancing biologics drug discovery through scalable models and data-driven pipelines — integrating generative models, structure prediction, and multi-parameter optimization with large-scale NGS datasets such as antibody repertoire sequencing and deep mutational scanning.

Before biologics, I spent years building graph-based genomics technology, leading the team behind GRAF — a variation-aware NGS platform that won the 2020 precisionFDA Truth Challenge against the likes of Google, Illumina, and Roche, and was named one of The Scientist's Top 10 Innovations of 2020. My PhD is in electrical engineering and physics, with a thesis on surface plasmon polaritons, photonics, and metamaterials. Antibodies, genome graphs, plasmons — different scales, same instinct for turning hard measurement problems into clean algorithms.
